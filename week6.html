Week 6 - Email Writing Etiquette

Session Details

Resource Person: Gowrisha Karuneswaran

Designation: Lecturer, Northern Uni

Topic: Email Writing Etiquette

Key Points

  • The session focused on mastering email writing etiquette for professional and personal communication.
  • It covered structuring emails, adapting tone to context, and practicing various scenarios like thank-you notes and formal business communication.
  • Activities involved writing emails for specific scenarios and arranging jumbled email components to understand proper format.

Email Writing Basics

  • Use a clear, descriptive subject line to convey the email’s purpose.
  • Follow a standard format: greeting, introduction, main content, closing, and signature.
  • Maintain a professional tone for business emails and a friendly tone for informal ones, adapting to the recipient and context.
  • Proofread for grammar, spelling, and clarity to ensure professionalism.

Email Scenarios

  • Thank You Email: Expressed gratitude to a professional contact for insights gained at a networking event, summarizing key takeaways.
  • Job Application Follow-Up: Inquired about the status of a job application post-interview, reinforcing interest in the position.
  • Informal Invitation: Invited friends to a casual get-together, specifying date, time, location, and instructions.
  • Request for Information: Asked a resort for details on cabin availability, amenities, and pricing for a getaway.
  • Apology Email: Acknowledged a project mistake affecting a colleague, explained the issue, and offered a solution.
  • Formal Business Communication: Informed a client about a product delivery delay, providing a revised timeline and apologizing.
  • Feedback Email: Reported defects in an online purchase to customer support, requesting a replacement or refund.
  • Condolences Email: Expressed sympathy to a colleague for a family loss, offering support.

Jumbled Email Activity

  • Practiced arranging jumbled email components (e.g., request, apology, closing) into a coherent time-off request email.
  • Correct order: formal request, specify date, mention fulfilled responsibilities, apologize for inconvenience, ask for procedures, thank for consideration, closing, signature.
  • Activity reinforced understanding of logical email structure and professional tone.

Email Etiquette Tips

  • Address recipients appropriately (e.g., "Dear [Name]" for formal emails, "Hi [Name]" for informal ones).
  • Keep emails concise, focusing on key points to respect the recipient’s time.
  • Use CC and BCC appropriately to include relevant parties without cluttering inboxes.
  • Respond promptly to emails to demonstrate professionalism and reliability.

Key Takeaways

  • I learned to structure emails effectively, adapting tone and content to suit various professional and personal contexts.
  • Practicing diverse scenarios improved my ability to communicate clearly and professionally via email.
  • Understanding email etiquette, such as concise writing and prompt responses, enhances workplace communication.
  • The jumbled email activity reinforced the importance of logical email organization and proper formatting.
