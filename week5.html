Week 5 - Meeting and Speaking Skills

Session Details

Resource Person: Gowrisha Karuneswaran

Designation: Lecturer, Northern Uni

Topic: Meeting and Speaking Skills

Key Points

  • The session focused on developing effective speaking and facilitation skills for conducting and participating in meetings.
  • It covered meeting terminology, types of meetings, planning strategies, and techniques for contributing productively.
  • Practical group activities, such as mock meetings and role assignments, enhanced confidence in professional communication.

Meeting Terminology

  • Learned key terms used in meetings through activities like matching exercises to understand their meanings and applications.
  • Terminology included roles (e.g., chair, secretary), documents (e.g., agenda, minutes), and actions (e.g., motion, adjourn).
  • Understanding these terms improves clarity and professionalism in meeting discussions.

Types of Meetings

  • Explored various meeting types, such as informational, decision-making, problem-solving, and team-building meetings.
  • Group discussions identified contexts and objectives for each type, enhancing awareness of their purposes.
  • Recognizing meeting types helps tailor communication and contributions effectively.

Planning a Meeting

  • Followed a preparation checklist to define the meeting’s purpose, desired outcomes, attendees, and logistics (e.g., date, venue, equipment).
  • Prepared essential documents like invitations, agendas, and minutes to ensure organized and productive meetings.
  • Anticipated potential problems to mitigate challenges and maintain meeting efficiency.

Contributing Effectively

  • Learned techniques to speak confidently, listen actively, and provide constructive input during meetings.
  • Practiced using clear, concise language to propose ideas, agree, or disagree respectfully.
  • Role-playing activities, such as facilitating discussions, improved engagement and collaboration skills.

Key Takeaways

  • I gained confidence in using meeting terminology and contributing effectively to discussions.
  • Planning and preparation are critical for conducting organized and productive meetings.
  • Practical activities like mock meetings enhanced my speaking, facilitation, and collaboration skills.
  • Evaluating meetings helps identify areas for improvement and ensures future success.
