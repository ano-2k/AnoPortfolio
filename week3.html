Week 3 - Interview Manners

Session Details

Resource Person: Gowrisha Karuneswaran

Designation: Lecturer, Northern Uni

Topic: Interview Manners

Key Points

  • The session focused on mastering interview manners to create a strong, professional impression on employers.
  • It covered preparation strategies, appropriate behavior, and post-interview etiquette to boost employability.
  • Practical exercises, such as mock interviews and certificate organization, reinforced professional conduct.

Preparing for the Interview

  • Research the company’s mission, values, products, and recent news to tailor responses and show interest.
  • Analyze the job description to align your skills and experiences with the role’s requirements.
  • Practice responses to common questions using the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result) to highlight achievements.
  • Prepare thoughtful questions about the role, team, or company culture to demonstrate engagement.

Professional Presentation

  • Dress in professional, well-fitted attire suited to the company’s culture, using neutral colors.
  • Maintain grooming standards with clean hair, trimmed nails, and minimal, tasteful accessories.
  • Use positive body language, including eye contact, a firm handshake, and an upright posture.
  • Apply subtle or no fragrances to avoid overwhelming interviewers.

Answering Common Questions

  • For “Tell me about yourself,” summarize your professional background, focusing on relevant experiences.
  • For “Why do you want to work here?” connect your career goals to the company’s mission or culture.
  • When discussing strengths, provide examples, such as leading teams to demonstrate communication skills.
  • For weaknesses, mention a minor area you’re improving, like taking a course to enhance a skill.

Handling Behavioral Questions

  • Use real-life examples to showcase problem-solving, teamwork, and communication skills.
  • Structure responses with the STAR method to clearly outline the situation, task, action, and result.
  • Emphasize soft skills like adaptability and leadership relevant to the role.

Organizing Your Certificate Folder

  • Use a professional folder with dividers to categorize certificates (e.g., education, professional certifications).
  • Arrange certificates chronologically, with the most recent first, and use sheet protectors for preservation.
  • Include a table of contents and tailor certificates to the job, bringing copies of key documents.

Do’s and Don’ts

  • Do: Research the company, practice responses, ask thoughtful questions, and send a thank-you email.
  • Don’t: Arrive late, overshare personal details, criticize past employers, or use inappropriate language.
  • Do: Maintain positive body language and highlight relevant skills; Don’t: Memorize answers or use your phone during the interview.

Key Takeaways

  • I learned how thorough preparation, including research and practice, enhances interview performance.
  • Professional presentation through attire, grooming, and body language creates a strong first impression.
  • The STAR method helps deliver clear, impactful responses to behavioral questions.
  • Mock interviews and certificate organization improved my confidence and preparedness for professional settings.
